Comilla is a district in the Chittagong division of Bangladesh, located approximately 100 km southeast of the capital, Dhaka. It is bordered by Brahmanbaria and Narayanganj to the north, Noakhali and Feni to the south, India's Tripura state to the east, and Munshiganj and Chandpur to the west. The district, which was renamed from Tippera in 1960, is known for its historical sites like the ancient Lalmai-Mainamati hills and a World War II cemetery, as well as for producing rosmalai (a sweet) and the indigenous "Comilla Khade" cloth. Geography and climate Area: Approximately 3,146.30 square kilometers. Rivers: Major rivers include the Gumti, Meghna, and Little Feni. Terrain: Mostly plain land, with some hills in the east, particularly the Lalmai Hills. Climate: Tropical climate with hot summers and cold winters. History The area was once part of the ancient Samatata region and later the Tripura State. It came under the control of the East India Company in 1765 and was established as Tippera district in 1790. The district was renamed Comilla in 1960. Demographics and culture Population: As of the 2022 census, the population was over 6.3 million. Religion: The majority of the population is Muslim. Notable landmarks: The ancient Mainamati War Cemetery, established for British Commonwealth soldiers killed during World War II. Shalban Bihar, an important archaeological site. Comilla Victoria College and Comilla Zilla School, two ancient educational institutions. Bangladesh Academy for Rural Development (BARD). Local specialties: Famous for its sweets, particularly Rosmalai from shops like Matri Bhandar, and Comilla Khade cloth. Notable figures: The district is the birthplace of poet Nawab Faizunnesa, and Kazi Nazrul Islam, the national poet of Bangladesh, spent time in the area.
Comilla Polytechnic Institute (CPI) is a government technical institute in Kotbari, Comilla, Bangladesh, established in 1962, that offers a four-year diploma in engineering under the Bangladesh Technical Education Board (BTEB). It provides courses in six technologies: Civil, Electrical, Mechanical, Computer Science & Engineering, Electronics, and Power Technology. The institute features academic buildings, workshops, laboratories, a library, an auditorium, hostels for students, and administrative facilities. Key features Establishment: Founded in 1962. Administration: A government polytechnic institute managed by the Bangladesh government and affiliated with the Bangladesh Technical Education Board (BTEB). Location: Situated in Kotbari, Comilla, in a hilly area. Programs: Offers a four-year diploma in engineering in various technologies. Technologies offered: Civil Technology Electrical Technology Mechanical Technology Computer Science & Engineering Electronics Technology Power Technology Facilities: Includes academic buildings, workshops, laboratories, a library, and a 500-capacity auditorium. Residential facilities: Has three hostel halls: two for male students and one for female students. Other amenities: Includes a canteen, a mosque, and a large field.
Bangladesh is a densely populated country in South Asia, characterized by its fertile delta formed by major rivers and a tropical monsoon climate. It is bordered by India and Myanmar, with a coastline along the Bay of Bengal. The capital and largest city is Dhaka, and its economy is significantly driven by garment exports. Historically a Muslim-majority country, Bangladesh is known for its rich culture, vibrant festivals, and role as the birthplace of modern microcredit. Geography and climate Bangladesh's landscape is a low-lying, riverine delta, making it susceptible to flooding and cyclones. The world's largest delta, the Ganges Delta, is located here, and the country's largest mangrove forest, the Sundarbans, is shared with India. It has a tropical monsoon climate with high temperatures, humidity, and heavy rainfall during monsoon season. Cox's Bazar, on the southern coast, is the world's longest unbroken natural sandy sea beach. Demographics and culture Bangladesh is one of the most densely populated countries in the world, with a population of over 171 million people. It is home to a predominantly Muslim population, with significant Hindu, Buddhist, and Christian minorities. Festivals, both religious and secular, are an integral part of the culture and include major celebrations like Eid-ul-Fitr, Durga Puja, and the New Year's festival, Pohela Boishakh. The country has six distinct seasons: Summer, Monsoon, Autumn, Late Autumn, Winter, and Spring.
